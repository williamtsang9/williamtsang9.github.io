Issues in Tech

11/1/14


Nowadays, internet is so readily available that whenever we have questions, we turn to the internet for answers. The internet has become our one stop shop for solutions to our wildest and most unexplained questions. In one hand, it is a great thing! The idea of people sharing their facts, thoughts and opinions with others provides more and more data for everyone to see! On the other hand, people are making it a habit to "google" the questions for the answers they are seeking. What does this mean for education?

Do you have gramatically incorrect errors in your essay? NO PROBLEM. AUTOCORRECT. Looking for solutions for the homework due tomorrow because you spent the entire weekend partying? NO PROBLEM. GOOGLE CAN HELP YOU FIND IT.The fact that we are now digitizing anything and everything, making material available to anyone on the web, can become a problem. The younger generation no longer places spelling as a priority because they know they can run spell check and correct their entire document. Students know they can probably find the exact homework problem and solution online so they are proned to copy and pasting answers without actually understanding the basis of the problem. Habits are taught and stick on to the mind when people are young. Imagine what would happen when they are asked to solve or do something WITHOUT the internet or computer.

Like they say, habits are hard to break.
